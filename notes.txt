Techniques from the slides that are used in the HTML
1. Quantitative visual encoding
The slides describe quantitative visualization as mapping data attributes into visual variables and spatial position. In your HTML, this is clearly happening:
angle/position encodes year
radius encodes numerical magnitude
color distinguishes variables
area/size is used for the population wedges
This matches the slide’s idea that visualizations use spatial mappings and visual variables to communicate data. 
2. Node size / size as meaning
In the layout-generation part of the slides, node size is used to represent actor prominence. Your HTML does something very similar, just with a different dataset:
larger population wedge radius means larger population
larger polygon radius means larger value for GDP, inflation, or unemployment
So the technique of encoding importance/value by size is definitely used. 
3. Circular / radial layout
One slide explains a layout where nodes are positioned on circular orbits in hyperbolic space. Your chart is also fundamentally organized around a circular radial structure:
years are placed around a circle
axes radiate from the center
values extend outward from the center
It is not the same mathematical model as HTLM, but the radial-orbit arrangement is clearly related. 
4. Layering of multiple variables in one view
The slides emphasize combining information into one visual language and using visualizations to reveal relationships efficiently. Your HTML overlays:
population wedges
GDP polygon
inflation polygon
unemployment polygon
That is a layered multi-variable representation in a single canvas, which fits the “combine information space and visualization space” idea from the slides. 
5. Labels and legend for readability
The lecture frames visualization as a communication tool, not just graphics. Your HTML includes:
year labels
numeric labels on nodes
a legend
a title
These are not fancy algorithms, but they are part of the slide’s broader visualization principle: making the message readable and understandable.
